Why does user research and UI/UX matter? Description: #FocusOnTheUser - User satisfaction is partner satisfaction and partner satisfaction is your satisfaction. Throughout your career as a developer, you will build products for people.

Throughout your career as a developer, you will build products for people. The success or failure of the product will be determined by the user adoption, experience, and feedback. It kinda sucks that your technical work in many cases is measured by how the end user feels when they interact with the final product or feature. This is the reality of building products. Ensuring smooth user experience by doing user research and building compelling and intuitive applications will lead to positive feedback and successful partner work. UI/UX is especially critical as it is the first thing that the Product Owner and users see, so it’s important to make a good impression.

During Simulations and Apprenticeship, you will be required to liaise with your POs on multiple occasions and defend the work you have done from a user perspective. On many occasions in your career, you will have to communicate ‘the technical’ to ‘non-technical’ human beings. They act as users and you will be required to continuously check in with them and iterate accordingly based on their feedback.
